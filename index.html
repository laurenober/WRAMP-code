Unlikely? impossible? - How do you visualize it?

When uncertainty, odds, and (im)probabilities are communicated to us, we perceive and express them in different ways depending on our culture and the language that we use. The goal of this survey is to learn how people perceive and represent expressions of (im)probabilities in everyday life.

Before you proceed, please read the following consent form carefully:

The goal of this research is to improve the communication of probabilistic information through data visualization.

Procedure: You will be presented a fictitious context, as well as the directives and questions to which you can answer either by interacting with the visualizations or by typing your answer in the provided area. Do NOT provide numerical answer, think of words and expressions instead!

What you will get out of it: You will expand your vocabulary related to probability and uncertainty, and learn about visual design and representations of uncertainty.

Duration: The experiment will take you at most 18 minutes.

Privacy: Your participation is anonymous and your responses cannot be used to identify you.
